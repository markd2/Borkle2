# Keep it simple

Try to keep data structures and algorithms, and use of language features, as simple as possible.

# Discussion

Borkle2 is planned to be deployed not only macstyles as the primary editing and viewing environment, but also as an embedded library powering other apps (like the InfoCommer).  There are also plans of taking Borkle2-generated content and displaying it on ancient systems, such as the Three-Rivers _PERQ_ and Vax/VMS.  These systems don't have Swift and all of its features (and complexity and overhead...) They might be implement in Pascal, or pre-K&R C, or something else.

These older systems should be able to open Borkle content, and make it usable.  Such as searching, filtering by tags, and on graphical systems (PERQ/X11/etc) maybe even make it browsable.

Because of this older system requirement, we shouldn't go too nutzoid with Swift langauge features, instead keeping it older school style
